# SweetEscape — brand context

This file is read by the `impeccable`, `frontend-design`, and `design-taste-frontend` skills so every generated artifact is steered toward the same voice, palette, and compositional language.

## One-line

SweetEscape is an artisanal fusion bakery in Chandigarh — classical French technique, Indian-ingredient flourishes, served out of a sun-warmed storefront on Sector 35-B.

## Brand

- **Name**: SweetEscape (one word, CamelCase when displayed; lowercase only inside code identifiers).
- **Location**: SCO 114, Sector 35-B, Chandigarh 160022.
- **Phone**: +91 172 419 7326.
- **Hours**: Tue–Sun, 08:00 – 22:00. Closed Mondays.
- **Founders**: Ishaan Sood (pastry chef, stages at Poilâne + Du Pain et des Idées) and Niharika Bhardwaj (founder, ex-hospitality).
- **Founded**: 2022.

## Audience

- 25–45-year-old Chandigarh urbanites.
- Couples strolling Sector 17 on weekends.
- Gifters of premium cakes and hampers.
- Corporate and luxury-hotel B2B orders.

## Voice

- **Warm, confident, slightly cheeky**. Never “artisanal buzzword soup.”
- Short, specific sentences. Prefers verbs ("we laminate," "we proof") to adjectives.
- Never uses: _"Welcome to our cozy little bakery,"_ _"We use only the finest ingredients,"_ _"Made with love."_
- Favoured turns of phrase: _"baked at 5am,"_ _"served warm,"_ _"the honest ones,"_ _"Chandigarh's morning ritual."_

## Differentiators

- **Fusion without gimmick**: cardamom in kouign-amann, saffron in tres leches, kahwa in chocolate torte. Indian ingredient, French structure.
- **Open bakery**: the kitchen is visible from the seating floor; guests watch lamination happen.
- **Small batches**: nothing is baked past lunch — afternoon pick-ups are pre-ordered.

### Typography (locked)

- **Display**: `Fraunces` — variable, optical sizing on, warm serif.
- **Body**: `DM Sans` — variable, geometric sans.
- **Script**: `Caveat` — sparingly, for signature stamps and handwritten moments.
- **Mono**: `JetBrains Mono` — prices, addresses, hours.

### Banned (per `design-taste-frontend`)

- No `Inter`, no `Roboto`, no `Poppins`, no default system stack.
- No purple/blue gradient (`violet-500 → indigo-500`); no dark theme toggle by default.
- No `h-screen` (use `min-h-[100dvh]`).
- No stock photography of smiling chefs; no generic "our story" hero.
- No generic names — use realistic Chandigarh personas and dishes.
- No animation of layout properties (only `transform` + `opacity`).

## Motion direction

Every page earns its motion. Scroll feels _composed_, not reactive:

- Horizontal scroll-hijack, pinned scrollytelling, kinetic marquees, parallax tilt, kinetic typography, procedural 3D pastries, drag-to-pan galleries, gooey cart morph.
- Honour `prefers-reduced-motion`: reveal content instantly, skip pinning, disable R3F auto-rotate.
